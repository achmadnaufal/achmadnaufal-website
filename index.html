Hello. I'm Naufal. This is a quiet page where I keep a short profile and, eventually, some notes.

I grew up in Indonesia and studied statistics in Surabaya. The first half of my working life was spent around pharma commercial analytics — regional sales dashboards, supply chains, the usual spreadsheet weather. More recently I've drifted toward forests and fieldwork, helping small teams make sense of the messy data that comes back from plots and villages across Indonesia, Thailand, and a few places in between.

I like things that are slow and legible: longhand notes, walks, a good map, code that reads like prose. Most of what I put online lives somewhere between a notebook and a letter — short, personal, not especially polished.

Currently

Reading about peatlands and how they refuse to be counted. Learning a little more QGIS every week. Trying to write more and ship less. Drinking too much kopi tubruk.
